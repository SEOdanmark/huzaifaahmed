It is important to (not) take any risks when dealing with the aftermath of a lightning strike. First, it is essential to determine whether anyone was injured by the lightning strike. Fire extinguishers should be on-hand in order to quickly put out any flames that may have started due to the lighting strike. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the procedure for responding to a lightning strike in Alabama?
Alabama tree service companies should first assess the damage caused by the strike and take necessary safety precautions. They should then contact local utility companies and determine whether the tree is still energized before pruning or removing it.
Are there any specific regulations when dealing with lightning-struck trees in Alabama?
Yes, all work related to lightning-struck trees must be done according to state and local laws. This includes having an arborist or other qualified professional inspect the tree prior to pruning or removal of branches and ensuring that all safety procedures are followed when working with energized power lines near the tree.
Is it safe to climb a lightning-struck tree in Alabama?
No, it is not safe for anyone to climb a lightning-struck tree due to potential danger from electrically charged branches or nearby power lines. Any work on a lightning-struck tree should be carried out by trained professionals who understand how to safely navigate these hazardous conditions.
What type of special equipment is needed for safely handling damaged trees after a lightning strike in Alabama?
Depending on the severity of damage, specialized tools such as nonconductive poles, protective clothing, climbing ropes, fall arrest systems, chainsaws, and pole saws may be required for safely cutting down or pruning branches from a damaged tree following a lightning strike.
